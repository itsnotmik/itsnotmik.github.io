About me

As a Machine Learning Engineer and Computer Science graduate, I specialize in developing innovative solutions at the intersection of artificial intelligence, environmental technology, and cybersecurity.

My research work focuses on Graph Convolutional Neural Networks with attention mechanisms for water treatment systems, where I've developed predictive models that identify causal relationships and detect operational faults. This US National Alliance for Water Innovation funded research has led to multiple forthcoming publications in prestigious journals including Desalination and Nature Scientific Data. Beyond research, I lead development teams and manage complex technical projects.

At Nanoinfo, I oversee a diverse team building a comprehensive nanomaterial database that tracks environmental impacts and toxicity profiles—combining data science with environmental consciousness. My technical expertise spans Python, Golang, and DevOps technologies, which I've leveraged to create projects ranging from Linux package managers to recommendation engines. I maintain a personal homelab infrastructure that hosts this portfolio, secure password management systems, and other services using modern cloud tunneling technology.

In cybersecurity competitions, I've proven my skills by ranking in the top 100 individuals (out of 600) in the National Cyber League's experienced bracket and helping lead my team to an 11th place finish among 460 teams.

I'm driven by the challenge of applying computational methods to solve real-world problems, particularly in areas where technology can improve sustainability and security. My approach combines rigorous technical knowledge with practical implementation skills and leadership experience.

I'm currently seeking opportunities to apply my expertise in machine learning, software engineering, and data science to projects that make a meaningful impact.
